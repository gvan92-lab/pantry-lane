Chapter one

How we got here.

It started in a kitchen in Brooklyn, with a bottle of olive oil that wasn't quite what the label promised. So we started asking questions: where does this stuff really come from? Who made it? Is it as good as it could be?

Turns out, the pantry — the part of the kitchen we use every single day — is full of compromises. Mass-produced staples, vague labels, prices that have nothing to do with quality. We thought we could do better.

So we started traveling. Talking to producers. Tasting everything. The shelf you see today is the result of a few hundred conversations and a lot of really good meals — and we're just getting started.

What we care about

Three rules
we don't break.

Every decision we make runs through these three filters. If a product can't pass all three, it doesn't make the shelf.

i.

People first.

We work directly with producers and pay them fairly. No middlemen squeezing margins, no anonymous supply chains. We can name every farmer, baker, and miller behind every item on this site.

ii.

Made well.

Time-honored methods, real ingredients, no shortcuts. If we wouldn't put it on our own table, it doesn't go on yours. Better is always better than more.

iii.

Transparent.

Every product has a story — and we tell it, in full. You'll always know where your food came from, who made it, and why we picked it.

Where we are

Small on purpose. Better by design.

We could be twice as big and half as good. Instead we're going slow, adding things only when we find something genuinely worth your shelf space. The good stuff takes time.
